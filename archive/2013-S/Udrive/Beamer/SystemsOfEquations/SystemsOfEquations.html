Algorithm for solving \( 2 \times 2\) homogeneous

  • Obtain coefficient matrix \( A \)
  • Find eigenvalues: the roots of \( \det(A - \lambda I) \)
  • Solve for the coefficient vectors: the eigenvectors of \( A \)

Example

Consider the system of differential equations In matrix form, this becomes \[ \vec{x}' = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 4 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \vec{x}. \] Write \( A \) for the coefficient matrix.

  • Check that \( \det{A - \lambda I} = \lambda^2 - 2 \lambda -3 \), so that the eigenvalues of \( A \) are \( \lambda_1 = 3 \), \( \lambda_2 = -1 \).
  • Solve the modified system for each eigenvalue to obtain eigenvectors.

Getting the eigenvectors

The modified system is \[ \begin{pmatrix} 1 - \lambda & 1 4 & 1 - \lambda \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} \xi_1 \\ \xi_2 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}. \]

  • When \( \lambda = 3 \), we see that the system reduces to the equation \( -2 \xi_1 + \xi_2 = 0 \), so an eigenvector for \( \lambda = 3 \) is \[ \vec{\xi}^{(1)} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix}. \]
